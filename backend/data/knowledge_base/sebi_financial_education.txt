SEBI Investor Education and Awareness

Recommended Savings Rate:
A general rule of thumb for individuals is to save 20-30% of their monthly income. This ensures enough for future goals and emergencies.

Emergency Fund:
An emergency fund should ideally cover 3 to 6 months of essential living expenses. This fund should be kept in liquid forms like savings accounts or liquid mutual funds for easy access during unforeseen events.

The 50/30/20 Rule:
A popular budgeting method recommended for many is to allocate 50% of income to needs (essentials like rent, food, bills), 30% to wants (lifestyle, dining, entertainment), and 20% to savings and debt repayment.

Systematic Investment Plan (SIP):
SIP is a disciplined way of investing in mutual funds. Starting early and being consistent with small amounts can lead to significant wealth creation due to the power of compounding. For example, starting an SIP at 25 instead of 35 can significantly increase the terminal value of investments.

Insurance Recommendations:
Adequate life and health insurance are essential for financial security. Health insurance covers medical emergencies, and life insurance provides for dependents in case of the policyholder's demise. It is recommended to have a term life insurance cover of at least 10-15 times annual income.

Financial Goals:
Identify and categorize goals into short-term (1-3 years), medium-term (3-7 years), and long-term (7+ years). This helps in choosing the right investment instruments.
